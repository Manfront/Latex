\textbf{Boundary Value Theorem:}

Let $f(x)$ be continuous on the closed interval $[a, b]$ and differentiable on the open interval $(a, b)$. If $f(x)$ satisfies the conditions:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item $f(a)$ and $f(b)$ exist.
    \item There exists $c \in (a, b)$ such that $f'(c) = \dfrac{f(b) - f(a)}{b - a}$.
\end{enumerate}
Then, there exists $c \in (a, b)$ such that $f(c) = \dfrac{f(b) - f(a)}{b - a}$.
